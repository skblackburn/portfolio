### The Carl‑Kay Café Menu Project

<br>

**The Carl‑Kay Café** is a personal accessibility project I created for my family. My brother has Down syndrome and cannot read or communicate clearly, so images are essential for helping him express his wants. My fiancé also chooses food visually when ordering from menus. Over the years, I collected and printed hundreds of recipes and stored them in large binders so I could cook without relying on screens that go dark or get messy.

While the binders worked for cooking, they were overwhelming for meal planning. My family needed a simple, visual way to choose meals for the week. To solve this, I designed a fully illustrated, restaurant‑style menu called **The Carl‑Kay Café**, named after our middle names. Each dish includes a photo and a short description, allowing my brother and fiancé to browse options just like a real menu.

The final menu spans more than fifty pages and organizes our recipes into categories such as **appetizers, salads, soups, sandwiches, brunch, desserts, breads, pastries, and main dishes organized by cuisine**. It lives in a small binder that my family can flip through easily, and it has become our weekly meal‑planning tool. Once selections are made, I use the original recipe binders to prepare the grocery list and cook the meals.

This project reflects my focus on **accessibility**, **visual communication**, and **user‑centered design**—creating tools that meet people where they are and make everyday tasks easier and more enjoyable.
