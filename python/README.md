## My Approach to Building Python Tools

I’ve always been someone who sees systems before they exist. For years, I could look at a messy process and immediately understand how it could be cleaner, clearer, and more intuitive — but I didn’t have the technical means to build the tools I imagined. With the rise of AI, that changed. I finally have a way to turn the workflows I’ve carried in my head for years into something real and usable.

I’m not a traditional programmer, and I don’t pretend to be. My strength is understanding how a process should work, how data should flow, and how a tool should feel to the person using it. I design the logic, the structure, and the behavior of each system. AI helps me translate those designs into Python code.

Across the Python projects I’ve built:

- I designed the workflows, logic, and data structures.  
- I defined the rules, inputs, outputs, and processing steps.  
- I created the architecture and system behavior.  
- I used AI as a tool to help generate portions of the code that implement the logic I designed.  
- I validated the outputs, refined the logic, and ensured the tools solve real business problems.  

These projects reflect my ability to:

- break down complex business processes  
- design clear and intuitive workflows  
- create tools that solve real problems for real people  
- collaborate with AI to bring ideas to life  
- build systems that are practical, portable, and easy to use  

At my core, I’m someone who sees what’s possible, imagines systems that didn’t exist before, and uses technology to make those systems real.
